# How to Calculate Forklift Total Cost

**Want to know the real cost of owning a forklift?** It’s not just about the upfront price. The **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** includes everything from purchase or rental fees, maintenance, and energy costs to operator wages and unexpected repairs. Here's a quick breakdown of what you need to consider:

- **Capital Costs:**

 - New electric forklifts: Rs. 7,080,000–Rs. 10,680,000
 - Used forklifts: Rs. 2,376,000–Rs. 6,720,000
 - Rentals: Rs. 387,000–Rs. 479,000/month
 - Battery costs: Lead-acid (Rs. 480,000–Rs. 1,440,000), Lithium-ion (Rs. 4,080,000–Rs. 4,800,000)
- **Maintenance Costs:**

 - Routine servicing: Rs. 24,000 every 200 hours
 - Major overhauls: Rs. 360,000 after 2,000 hours
 - Tyre expenses: 12–15% of ownership costs
- **Operational Costs:**

 - Energy: Rs. 150,000–Rs. 600,000 annually for electric forklifts
 - Labour: Average operator salary in Pakistan is Rs. 954,829/year

**Quick Tip:** Electric forklifts may cost more upfront but save up to 40% in energy and maintenance costs over time. Renting is cheaper initially but can be expensive long-term.

## Capital Costs

When it comes to forklifts, capital costs make up the largest chunk of the initial expense. Whether you're buying a brand-new machine, opting for a used one, or going with a rental, understanding these costs is crucial. It helps you align your spending with your budget and operational goals.

### Purchase Price and Initial Investment

The cost of a forklift depends on several factors - its type, capacity, and whether it’s new or used. Electric forklifts typically carry a higher price tag upfront compared to diesel or LPG models. For instance:

- A **new 1,360 kg electric forklift** costs between **Rs. 8,760,000 and Rs. 9,480,000**, while the cushion tyre variant is slightly cheaper, ranging from **Rs. 7,080,000 to Rs. 7,680,000**.
- Larger **2,270 kg electric forklifts** are priced at **Rs. 8,880,000 to Rs. 10,680,000**, with cushion tyre models costing **Rs. 8,400,000 to Rs. 8,880,000**.

For those considering used forklifts, prices vary widely. A **1,360 kg electric model** can cost anywhere from **Rs. 2,376,000 to Rs. 6,720,000**, with an average price of around **Rs. 4,100,000 for electric models** and **Rs. 4,400,000 for cushion tyre versions**.

Don’t forget to factor in **import duties**, **taxes**, and **customs brokerage fees**. These additional costs can pile up quickly and should be accounted for to avoid unnecessary delays or penalties.

### Battery Costs for Electric Forklifts

If you’re considering electric forklifts, battery costs are a major part of the upfront investment. The type of battery you choose - lead-acid or lithium-ion - can significantly impact both your initial spending and long-term operations:

- **Lead-acid batteries** cost between **Rs. 480,000 and Rs. 1,440,000** each. However, they also require charging infrastructure, which adds to the expense.
- **Lithium-ion batteries**, though pricier at **Rs. 4,080,000 to Rs. 4,800,000**, offer clear advantages. They are 30% more energy-efficient, charge up to eight times faster, and have a lifespan two to four times longer. Just make sure to check compatibility and ballast requirements before investing.

## Maintenance and Service Costs

When calculating the total cost of owning forklifts, maintenance and service expenses are a major consideration. These costs can vary significantly depending on how you manage upkeep. A proactive, planned maintenance approach is generally more economical than reactive repairs. By understanding these costs, you can budget more effectively and avoid unpleasant surprises.

### Routine Maintenance Expenses

Routine maintenance is essential for keeping forklifts running smoothly. Interestingly, electric forklifts tend to cost about 14% less to maintain compared to internal combustion models. While the upfront cost of electric forklifts may be higher, their long-term savings make them a smart investment.

Monthly maintenance includes tasks like lubrication, filter and brake checks, and tyre inspections. Annual servicing is more intensive, covering engine or motor maintenance, replacing major components, and conducting thorough safety checks.

The frequency of maintenance depends on how much the forklift is used. For example, maintenance every 200 operating hours costs around Rs. 24,000, while a complete overhaul after 2,000 hours can cost Rs. 360,000. Keeping track of operating hours is crucial for accurate budgeting.

Tyres are another significant expense, making up 12–15% of ownership costs. Regular inspections and retreading can extend their lifespan. Fazlerasheed And Company offers retreading services that help tyres last longer, providing a cost-effective alternative to full replacements.

For electric forklifts, battery upkeep is another major factor. Lead-acid batteries need regular electrolyte checks and proper charging to stay in good shape. Fazlerasheed And Company also provides battery regeneration services, which can restore up to 80% of battery capacity, delaying costly replacements.

While routine maintenance helps prevent many issues, unexpected repairs and wear-and-tear can still arise.

### Unplanned Repairs and Wear-and-Tear

Unplanned repairs can be both disruptive and expensive. For example, downtime in a warehouse can cost as much as Rs. 2,400,000 per hour, while repair costs average Rs. 1,200,000 (or Rs. 150,000 per hour).

Several factors contribute to unplanned maintenance. Poor driving habits, such as speeding or hard braking, can accelerate wear and tear. Similarly, uneven surfaces, potholes, and poorly designed loading docks can cause unnecessary damage.

Using genuine spare parts is one way to reduce long-term repair costs. Authentic parts are specifically designed for your equipment, ensuring better performance and avoiding compatibility issues. Fazlerasheed And Company stocks high-quality spare parts to help you avoid repeated repairs.

Certain components require regular attention to prevent unexpected failures. These include:

- **Forks**: Check for cracks or excessive wear.
- **Hydraulic systems**: Monitor for leaks and ensure oil quality.
- **Masts**: Tighten nuts and bolts and keep them well-lubricated.
- **Brakes**: Inspect for proper functioning and pad wear.

### Planned Maintenance Contracts

Planned maintenance contracts turn unpredictable repair costs into fixed monthly expenses. These agreements help identify and resolve potential problems early, preventing them from escalating into costly repairs. Regular maintenance can reduce downtime by 30% to 50%, and professional servicing can lower repair costs by up to 25%.

Such contracts also provide transparency, making it easier to plan your budget while minimising the risk of unexpected downtime. A proactive maintenance strategy is almost always more economical.

When evaluating maintenance contracts, keep in mind factors like travel costs, after-hours rates, and potential surcharges. Typically, Planned Maintenance (PM) covers scheduled servicing but excludes replacement parts, while Full Maintenance (FM) includes comprehensive upkeep at a fixed monthly rate, with specific exclusions.

## Operational and Energy Costs

When it comes to forklifts, operational costs like energy, labour, and other recurring expenses can make up a large chunk of the total expenditure. These costs aren't just limited to fuel or electricity; they also include wages and other overlooked factors. Knowing these costs upfront can help businesses make smarter choices about the type of forklift they need and how to manage operations more efficiently.

### Energy and Fuel Costs

The cost of energy depends on the type of forklift and local electricity or fuel rates. For electric forklifts, electricity is the primary expense, while diesel or LPG forklifts require regular fuel purchases.

In Pakistan, electricity rates vary significantly based on the supplier and usage levels. Industrial electricity prices in 2024 averaged **13.5 cents per kWh**, which is nearly double the rates in countries like China, India, and the United States. These rates are regulated by the National Electric Power Regulatory Authority (NEPRA) and fluctuate due to factors like fuel costs, subsidies, and inefficiencies in the power grid. For instance, LESCO charges **PKR 10 per unit** for the first 100 units, but the cost jumps to **PKR 42–47 per unit** for usage above 700 units. Similarly, FESCO's rates range from **PKR 22 per unit** for the first 100 units to **PKR 42 per unit** for higher consumption brackets. Businesses can save on costs by scheduling forklift charging during off-peak hours, as electricity is pricier during peak demand times.

Another way to cut down on energy expenses is through battery energy storage systems (BESS). Pakistan imported approximately **1.25 gigawatt-hours of BESS in 2024**, showing a growing interest in this technology. ### Operator Wages and Labour Costs

Forklift operator wages in Pakistan vary by city, experience, and job conditions. On average, operators earn **PKR 954,829 annually** or about **PKR 459 per hour**. However, this can differ significantly by location. Operators with certifications, such as OSHA Forklift Operator training, usually earn more and are often more efficient and safer. ## Total Lifecycle Cost Analysis

When it comes to managing forklift expenses, looking at the big picture is crucial. A full view of the total cost of ownership (TCO) helps businesses make smarter decisions and plan budgets effectively. By combining all costs - capital, maintenance, and operational - into one figure, you can better understand the true expense of owning and operating a forklift over its entire lifespan.

### Compiling Total Cost of Ownership

The TCO calculation covers everything tied to running and maintaining a forklift throughout its life. The formula is straightforward: **TCO = acquisition cost + (related costs × years of use) – resale value**. If you plan to sell the forklift later, deduct its estimated resale value to get a clearer picture of your final TCO.

Each cost category plays a key role in the overall calculation:

| **Cost Category** | **Factors to Include** |
| --- | --- |
| **Capital Costs** | Purchase price, battery costs (for electric models), rental costs |
| **Maintenance Costs** | Routine servicing, unplanned repairs, maintenance contracts |
| **Operational Costs** | Energy or fuel expenses, operator wages, and additional charges |

One striking insight: operator wages can make up as much as 77% of the TCO. It’s also worth noting that a forklift with a lower upfront price may end up costing more in the long run due to frequent repairs or reduced lifespan.

### Lifecycle Cost Comparison

Once you've compiled all costs, comparing lifecycle expenses can help identify when replacing a forklift becomes the more economical choice. Lifecycle cost analysis shows when an asset's operating costs start to outweigh its value. Ideally, you want to spread capital costs over the longest possible period before yearly TCO begins to rise.

For example, a five-year analysis might reveal that electric forklifts can save up to 40% in operating costs compared to internal combustion models. To ensure accuracy, group similar equipment and track their maintenance, downtime, energy, and capital costs. Use depreciation to estimate residual values and adjust historical costs to current-year values by factoring in inflation.

## FAQs

### What’s the best way to decide between renting or buying a forklift for my business?

When deciding between renting or buying a forklift, it all boils down to your business requirements, how often you'll use the equipment, and your budget. Renting is ideal if you have short-term or seasonal needs. It saves you from hefty upfront costs and often includes maintenance in the rental fee, making it a hassle-free option.

On the flip side, buying a forklift makes more sense for long-term use. Ownership allows you to claim depreciation, spread costs over time, and potentially lower your overall expenses in the long run.

To choose wisely, think about how long you'll need the forklift, assess your cash flow, and consider whether you have the resources to handle maintenance. Carefully weighing these factors will help you find the most cost-efficient option for your business in Pakistan.

Investing in a **lithium-ion battery** for your electric forklift can be a game-changer for your business. While the upfront cost may be higher, the long-term benefits make it a worthwhile choice. These batteries are built to last, often surpassing 2,500 charge cycles, which means fewer replacements and reduced overall ownership expenses.

What sets lithium-ion batteries apart is their energy efficiency. They consume less electricity, cutting down on power bills, and charge much faster than traditional batteries. This not only reduces downtime but also boosts productivity and trims labour costs. Plus, they require minimal maintenance, saving you even more time and money in the long run.

For businesses in Pakistan, these features translate into a stronger **return on investment (ROI)**. With lower operating costs, improved efficiency, and exceptional durability, lithium-ion batteries are a smart and forward-thinking choice for electric forklifts.
